# FOA Alignment Memo

## Sponsor
- RFA: `DE-FOA-0003612`
- Program: **The Genesis Mission: Transforming Science and Energy with AI**
- Topic: **2 – Scaling the Biotechnology Revolution**
- Focus Area: **A. Biomolecular Science (BER)**

## Confirmed Sponsor Facts
- Phase I projects are **9 months**.
- Phase I expected award size is **$500,000–$750,000**.
- **Multi-institutional teams are required**.
- Phase I teams must include partner institutions from at least **two** of the following categories:
  - DOE/NNSA National Laboratory or Scientific User Facility
  - Industry
  - IHE / Non-profit / Other
- Phase I should demonstrate a **clear, tangible research workflow** and provide **quantitative analysis** of whether the approach is on a trajectory toward transformative capability.
- Topic 2 focuses on AI for biotechnology and embedded models that connect biological data across scales.
- Focus Area 2A emphasizes determining **fundamental principles connecting protein structure with function**.

## Proposed Project Fit
The proposed H5N1 project fits 2A because it centers on structure-informed modeling of how viral sequence variation alters biomolecular behavior relevant to:
- antigenic evolution
- immune escape
- host adaptation

The application should therefore emphasize:
- protein structure–function interpretation
- biomolecular interfaces and mutational effects
- integration of sequence, structural, and functional evidence
- AI-enabled scientific insight, not only prediction accuracy

## What the proposal should not become
To remain responsive, the proposal should not drift into:
- a broad public-health surveillance system proposal
- a purely epidemiologic transmission model
- a generic sequence-only virus classifier
- a vaccine deployment or clinical trial proposal
- a biodefense operations proposal without biomolecular science at the center

## Required proposal posture for Phase I
The proposal should present Phase I as:
- a scoped demonstration
- a benchmarked AI-enabled workflow
- a reproducible scientific pipeline
- a generator of evidence for Phase II scale-up

## Drafting implications
The narrative should explicitly answer:
1. What biomolecular principles are being modeled?
2. What AI methods enable the workflow?
3. What is the tangible Phase I workflow?
4. How will AI advantage be measured?
5. Why is DOE/BER the right home for this work?
