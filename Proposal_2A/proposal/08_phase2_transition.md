# Phase II Transition Logic

## What Phase I should de-risk
- feasibility of a unified structure-informed H5N1 biomolecular modeling workflow
- measurable gains over simpler baselines
- interpretable linkage between mutations, structural context, and modeled outcomes
- readiness for broader data/model scaling and stronger experimental coupling

## What Phase II could add
- expanded viral and host-interface datasets
- deeper multimodal integration
- broader antigenic and adaptation benchmarking
- tighter coupling to experimental validation or high-throughput biomolecular assays
- more advanced model scaling and deployment within Genesis Mission infrastructure

## Phase II decision criterion
Phase II should be justified only if Phase I demonstrates quantitative and scientifically interpretable evidence that AI materially improves biomolecular inference relative to conventional alternatives.
